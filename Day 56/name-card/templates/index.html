Intro

I was born in a small town in Eastern Central Washington called Wenatchee. After graduating high school in 2014 and attending Wenatchee Valley College for a year, I spent a year and a half participating in a fire science/fire-fighting residency program in which I responded alongside professional fire-fighters to emergency medical calls, structure fires, motor vehicle crashes, and much more. After attending an in-house recruit academy for the fire department, I decided that I wanted to do something different than firefighting. I returned to WVC in winter 2017 and applied to transfer to University of Washington. I was accepted to UW and began attending in Autumn quarter of 2017 after I finished my Associates Degree at WVC.

I initially hoped to pursue a degree in Chemical Engineering at UW, but I discovered that I did not like physics enough to do so. (I've been kicking myself ever since for not simply spending another year at WVC taking the prerequisite physics courses to avoid the weed-out classes at UW.) I also had a poor experience with a math teacher in Math 309, Linear Analysis; although I did alright in the course, receiving a 2.9 final grade, I found the teacher to be unhelpful and condescending during office hours. I had high hopes to pursue more math, but that class turned me off. I wanted to pursue Computer Science, but my grades weren't high enough in the prerequisite classes to be competitive. I took an elective in Winter of 2018 on the history of the Roman Republic, which left a significant impact on me. I enjoyed the thesis driven, argumentative writing, cultural study, reading primary sources, and asking questions to better understand the past. After a brief aspiration to major in Applied Math/Computational Science, I decided to major in History. I took courses in the History of the Salem Witch Trials, the History of Mughal India, the History of the Roman Empire, the History of Freedom in Ancient Rome and the Modern World, US Military History, and the History of the Modern Persian Gulf. I also discovered a passion for studying Latin and took two years of it. Ultimately, my education in Latin and Roman History allowed me to study two Roman agricultural texts for my Undergraduate Honors Thesis. For this work, I analyzed the role of the mos maiorum, the bonus agricola, and the symbolism of farming in two agricultural texts and the larger Roman cultural discourse to argue that these author's writings reflected their times and to suggest that one in particular predicted the rise of Augustan reactionism approximately 120 years before it occurred. After I graduated from UW in spring of 2020, I performed odd jobs and earned the CompTIA A+ certification. I joined the IT Help Desk at Confluence Health in Wenatchee in November of 2020 and have continued to work their in the same role since. In July of 2021, I began pursuing my Bachelor's of Science in Cybersecurity at Western Governors University, which I hope to earn by January of 2024.

Work

I have worked at Confluence Health at the IT Help since November 2020. For the last seven months, I helped the Identity and Access Management Team with account creation, privilege auditing, and other tasks.

About

I am an avid reader, writer, and thinker. I enjoy discussing literature, history, philosophy, science, and pretty much any other intellectual topic. I love learning more about people and making new friends. My interests include programming, writing, reading, studying history and language, hiking, fitness, cooking, backpacking, and much more.
